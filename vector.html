What is VECTOR

Not a startup club. A build environment.

VECTOR connects CCNY students with real clients who have real technical problems. Teams scope the project, build the software, and deliver something that works — developing professional habits that coursework alone can't teach.

"Your knowledge gap is your money maker. An NYC restaurant owner knows food, not Python. You know Python — and that gap is precisely what they'll pay you to close."
The insight behind VECTOR's approach to client work

How a project moves

From problem to portfolio artifact

Every VECTOR project follows the same arc. The goal is not just to ship software — it's to leave behind documentation, reflection, and proof of work that both the client and student can point to.

01

Problem intake

Find a real operational pain point from a business, club, or local initiative.

02

Scope sprint

Define the smallest useful version and clarify exactly what is out of scope.

03

Build team

Match students across engineering, design, product, and client communication.

04

Validate

Test the workflow with users, fix gaps, and verify it solves the stated problem.

05

Artifact

Turn the finished work into a case study, demo, and GitHub repo — documented and owned.

For Businesses

What working with VECTOR looks like

VECTOR teams build focused, well-scoped software for NYC businesses and organizations. If you have a process that should be automated, data that should be visible, or a digital presence that doesn't exist yet — let's talk.

The value proposition

Serious technical talent at a fraction of agency cost

VECTOR teams are CCNY students advised by faculty with industry experience. You get a clearly scoped project, a dedicated point of contact, and a finished deliverable — not a running hourly tab.

Transparent scope

You know exactly what you're getting

Every project starts with a written scope document — deliverables, timeline, what's included, and what isn't. No surprises, no scope creep, no ambiguity.

Local and accountable

A team in your city, not a contractor overseas

VECTOR teams are based in New York City. They understand the market, can meet in person, and are invested in the outcome because it goes in their portfolio.

What we build

Websites, dashboards, automations, AI tools

If your business has a manual process that could be automated, a spreadsheet that should be a dashboard, or needs an online presence — that's in our wheelhouse.

Ongoing support

We don't just hand it off and disappear

Clients can stay on a monthly maintenance retainer for updates, fixes, and small additions — so the software stays useful long after launch.
